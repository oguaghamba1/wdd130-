My Favorite City

My favorite city is Owerri, Imo state in Nigeria. It's where we lived before moving to the Uk. Wonderful people and very welcoming. Most of my family members still live there up till today. We had a company called Snow White Bottling Company that produces 18.9 ltr bottled water for dispenser machines. We lived at Federal Housing Estate, Egbeada, in an 8 bedroom duplex we built.

Owerri has a really lively atmosphere, especially around the main town areas and markets. What I remember most is how welcoming people are and how easy it is to feel at home there. It is also a city with strong family and community life, and when I think of Owerri I think of good food, familiar faces, and that sense of connection. Each time I visit, I notice small changes and development, which makes it feel like a place that keeps moving forward while still holding on to its identity.

Owerri also has a growing presence of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. It is encouraging to see how the Church is strengthening individuals and families through worship, service, and community activities. The city itself is part of Imo State and is known for being busy and populated, with people from different backgrounds coming in for education, business, and work. That mix of culture, faith, and daily life is one of the reasons Owerri still feels like an important place to me.

There are two stakes in the city, the Owerri Nigeria Stake and the Owerri Nigeria North Stake. I like that it gives members opportunities to worship, serve, and support each other locally, and it is something I always notice when I am thinking about community and faith life in Owerri. You can learn more about the Church on the official website.
